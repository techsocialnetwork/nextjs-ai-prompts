# From Data Analyst to AI Specialist: My 23-Year Journey

I'm not a career changer—I'm a career evolver. With 23 years in data analysis, a BCS Data Analyst Level 4 Distinction, and now IBM AI certification, here's how I'm transitioning from traditional BI to AI implementation.

## My Foundation: 23 Years in Data

### Early Career

Started in data analysis before "data science" was a buzzword. Spent years in enterprise environments:
- **Provident Financial**: HR MI Analyst, working with executive stakeholders
- **Morrisons**: Board-level reporting and strategic analysis
- **Various enterprises**: Dashboard development, forecasting, stakeholder management

### BCS Data Analyst Level 4 - Distinction (2020)

Completed the BCS apprenticeship with **Distinction**—the highest grade possible.

**Assessment highlights:**
- "Demonstrated almost all competencies at significantly higher level than PASS"
- Advanced statistical modeling: Structural Equation Modeling (SEM), ARIMA forecasting
- Strong stakeholder management and project delivery
- Business context understanding

**Key technical skills validated:**
- Statistical rigor: Bartlett sphericity, KMO tests
- Predictive modeling: Auto-ARIMA in RStudio
- Advanced visualization: Geospatial overlays, mosaic plots
- Project management: Taking analysis from start to finish

This wasn't just dashboard building—it was validated statistical modeling at distinction level.

## The Evolution: Adding AI

### Why AI Now?

After 23 years, I recognized the pattern:

> **"Understand complex systems → Explain them clearly → Build tools that make them accessible"**

From Power BI dashboards to TSN articles to IBM course notes—I've always translated complexity into actionable insight. AI is the next evolution of that pattern.

### IBM AI Foundations Certification

**27 courses. 64.5 hours. Complete specialization.**

**What I learned:**
- Generative AI and Large Language Models
- AI ethics, bias mitigation, and governance
- Machine learning and deep learning fundamentals
- Natural Language Processing and Computer Vision
- AI business strategy and implementation frameworks

**Key insight:** AI isn't replacing data analysis—it's amplifying it. The same statistical rigor I applied to ARIMA models now applies to LLM evaluation and AI system design.
